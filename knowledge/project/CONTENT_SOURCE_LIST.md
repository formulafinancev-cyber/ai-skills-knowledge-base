# CONTENT SOURCE LIST

## Purpose
This file defines the types of sources that the Formula Finance content system should pay attention to.

Its purpose is to guide content research, content intelligence, and content planning so that ideas and patterns come from relevant places, not from random noise.

## Main Principle
Content should be informed by what is really happening in the market and in the minds of business owners and managers.

Good sources help:
- see how people talk about financial and management problems;
- notice recurring pains and questions;
- understand how topics are framed in practice;
- avoid living in an isolated bubble.

This file defines source categories, not specific URLs.

## Source Categories

### 1. Educational / Expert Content on Finance and Management
Includes:
- articles about financial visibility;
- expert materials on management reporting and KPIs;
- practical finance and analytics blogs;
- materials on dashboards and business intelligence.

Purpose:
- to see how financial and analytical topics are explained;
- to understand what patterns and misconceptions are common;
- to refine how Formula Finance explains similar themes.

---

### 2. Business Owner and Manager Discussions
Includes:
- discussions where owners and managers talk about problems with numbers, reports, or visibility;
- Q&A threads;
- comments under practical materials;
- long-form posts describing real business difficulties.

Purpose:
- to hear real language used by decision makers;
- to spot what they find confusing or painful;
- to match content themes with real questions.

---

### 3. Market and Competitor Communication
Includes:
- how other financial and analytical services present themselves;
- their content topics;
- their messaging patterns;
- strengths and weaknesses in how they explain value.

Purpose:
- to understand the communication environment;
- to avoid copying;
- to see where Formula Finance can be clearer or more structured.

---

### 4. Regulatory and Economic Signal Summaries
Includes:
- digests of significant tax or regulatory changes;
- macroeconomic overview pieces;
- practical explanations of “what this regulatory change may mean” for businesses.

Purpose:
- to inspire content that connects regulatory/economic changes with management and reporting implications;
- to support the Legal & Regulatory Advisor with communication-friendly framing.

---

### 5. Internal Cases and Patterns
Includes:
- anonymized internal case patterns (see CASE_LIBRARY);
- recurring problem types observed in practice;
- examples of “before / after” in reporting and KPIs.

Purpose:
- to create content grounded in realistic situations;
- to avoid theoretical or purely speculative topics.

---

### 6. Tools, Systems, and Practices Around Reporting and Analytics
Includes:
- discussions and materials about reporting tools and dashboards;
- articles on KPI design and reporting best practices;
- content on practical use of analytics in managing a business.

Purpose:
- to inform content about the broader ecosystem;
- to connect Formula Finance’s structured approach with what people already use or know of.

---

## Role of Content Strategist / Content Intelligence
The content intelligence function should use these source categories to:

- identify themes that repeat;
- detect new relevant topics;
- observe language and framing that resonate;
- avoid copying, but extract patterns and insights.

The goal is not to replicate external content, but to understand the environment.

---

## Role of the “Content Scout” / Parsing Agent
A content-scouting or parsing agent (e.g., reading Telegram channels, social media, or local platforms) should:

- focus on sources that match these categories;
- track what types of topics and formats gain engagement;
- extract themes, angles, and styles — not copy text;
- support the planning of content that fits Formula Finance’s positioning and brand voice.

---

## Role of Publisher
The Publisher can use this list to:

- know which kind of external context matters;
- understand what types of reference material may be useful;
- coordinate with Content Strategist on what should be brought into the content plan.

---

## Brand Voice Alignment
Whatever the source, Formula Finance content should always be:

- clear;
- structured;
- calm;
- professional;
- practical.

External source tone should not override internal brand voice.

---

## Internal vs External Content
This file is about **where ideas and signals come from**, not about copying content.

External content is a source of:
- themes;
- questions;
- patterns;
- examples.

It is not a source of text to reproduce.

---

## Future Expansion
This file can later be expanded with:

- specific platform notes (e.g., which channels or site types are most useful);
- by-channel focus (e.g., what to look for in each major platform);
- specific content-scouting patterns for the content intelligence agent.

---

## Final Principle
Content sources for Formula Finance should help the project stay close to:

- real business pains;
- real language of decision makers;
- real patterns in the market.

They are there to inform structured, original content that matches the company’s positioning and brand voice.
