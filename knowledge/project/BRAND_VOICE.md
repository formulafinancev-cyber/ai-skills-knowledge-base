# BRAND VOICE

## Purpose
This file defines the brand voice for Formula Finance.

Its purpose is to keep all written and spoken communication consistent across:
- proposals;
- CRM communication;
- content;
- summaries;
- explanations;
- internal and external messages.

## Main Principle
The brand voice should reflect what Formula Finance is:

- structured;
- intelligent;
- practical;
- calm;
- serious about business;
- focused on clarity.

Communication should help people understand, not impress them with unnecessary complexity.

## Core Voice Attributes

### 1. Clear
The voice should be easy to understand.

Characteristics:
- simple, direct sentences;
- minimal jargon;
- explanations when a term may be unclear;
- structured presentation of ideas.

### 2. Structured
The voice should feel organized.

Characteristics:
- logical order;
- clear sections;
- visible structure;
- explicit transitions.

Communication should feel like it brings order, not adds noise.

### 3. Calm
The tone should be calm and steady.

Characteristics:
- no hype;
- no exaggerated promises;
- no pressure language;
- no panic.

Formula Finance should come across as a stable partner, not as a loud advertiser.

### 4. Professional
The voice should reflect professional seriousness.

Characteristics:
- respectful phrasing;
- no slang in formal contexts;
- no informal over-familiarity in business materials;
- balanced level of formality.

Professional does not mean cold, but it does mean careful and responsible.

### 5. Practical
The communication should focus on real usefulness.

Characteristics:
- focus on what actually helps the reader;
- examples grounded in reality;
- attention to decisions and actions;
- minimal abstract talking without application.

## Things to Avoid

The voice should avoid:
- hype terms without substance;
- empty buzzwords;
- aggressive sales language;
- exaggerated claims;
- overcomplicated technical talk when it does not help.

It should also avoid:
- sounding like a generic AI system;
- sounding like any “consultant for everything”.

## Typical Communication Contexts

### Proposals
Tone:
- calm;
- structured;
- confident but not aggressive;
- focused on clarity of scope, value, and next steps.

### CRM Communication
Tone:
- attentive;
- respectful;
- clear;
- non-pushy;
- focused on usefulness and next steps when appropriate.

### Content
Tone:
- educational;
- explanatory;
- insight-oriented;
- grounded in real business situations;
- not overly theoretical.

### Internal Explanations
Tone:
- direct;
- structured;
- rationale-oriented;
- supportive of project clarity and decisions.

## Voice Examples (Conceptual)

Desired feeling:
- “This helps me understand my business better.”
- “This is clear and structured.”
- “They know what they are doing, and they are serious about it.”
- “They are not trying to oversell, they are trying to explain.”

Undesired feeling:
- “This is hype with no content.”
- “This sounds like everyone else.”
- “This is confusing or full of buzzwords.”
- “I am not sure what they are actually offering.”

## Role-Specific Notes

### Colleague
Should:
- preserve brand voice even in more casual assistance;
- stay clear, calm, and structured;
- avoid becoming too informal when the context is business-related.

### Content Strategist and Publisher
Should:
- use this brand voice as the baseline for all external content;
- adapt slightly by channel if needed, but keep core attributes.

### Proposal Agent
Should:
- follow this voice closely in all offer documents;
- favor clarity and seriousness over decorative language.

### CRM Agent
Should:
- use this voice in all written CRM-related suggestions and communication drafts.

## Flexibility Rule
The voice can be slightly adjusted depending on context (e.g., more relaxed in internal notes, more formal in proposals), but the core attributes must remain consistent:

- clear;
- structured;
- calm;
- professional;
- practical.

## Final Principle
Brand voice is not decoration.

It is part of how Formula Finance expresses its core value: bringing clarity and structure to financial and analytical questions in a calm, professional, and useful way.
