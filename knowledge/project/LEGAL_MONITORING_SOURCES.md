# LEGAL MONITORING SOURCES

## Purpose
This file defines the concept of legal and regulatory monitoring sources for the Formula Finance project.

Its purpose is to give the Legal & Regulatory Advisor role a structured starting point for how to think about sources, even if specific URLs and systems are added later.

## Main Principle
Legal and regulatory monitoring should not be based on random fragments.

The system should have:
- a clear idea of where relevant information may come from;
- a structured way of thinking about source types;
- an understanding that not all sources are equally authoritative.

This file describes the categories and principles, not specific implementation details.

## Source Categories

### 1. Official Legal and Regulatory Sources
These are primary sources such as:
- laws;
- official regulations;
- official tax guidance;
- official regulatory updates;
- government publications.

These sources are the most authoritative and should carry the highest weight.

### 2. Official Explanatory and Interpretive Materials
These include:
- official commentaries;
- official explanatory notes;
- official Q&A documents;
- official guidelines.

They help interpret how rules are intended to be understood and applied.

### 3. Professional Summaries and Analyses
These include:
- professional tax and legal review articles;
- analytical summaries by specialized organizations;
- structured digests prepared by experts.

These are useful but are not primary law.  
They should support understanding, not replace direct sources.

### 4. News and Media
These include:
- business news;
- financial and economic news;
- major regulatory change announcements.

News can signal that something is changing, but should be cross-checked against more authoritative sources for details.

### 5. Internal Notes and Context
These include:
- internal interpretations;
- practical notes about how specific changes affect Formula Finance and its clients;
- internal guidelines for applying public rules in a business context.

These are important for internal operation, but should always reference external authoritative bases.

## Monitoring Philosophy
The Legal & Regulatory Advisor should:

- prioritize authoritative sources;
- treat secondary sources as helpful, but not definitive;
- be cautious with news headlines;
- aim to understand both the letter of the rule and its practical business impact.

The role is not only to “see the law”, but also to understand what it may mean for the business.

## Signal Types
Monitoring should pay attention to:

- tax changes;
- financial regulation changes;
- reporting requirements;
- compliance-related changes;
- significant macroeconomic regulatory measures;
- important sector-specific regulatory updates when relevant.

The Advisor should be able to:
- detect potentially important signals;
- flag them for attention;
- support basic explanation.

## Communication Role
The Legal & Regulatory Advisor is expected to:

- highlight important changes;
- explain them in clear language;
- indicate when something may affect the business or its clients;
- suggest when deeper professional legal or tax review is needed beyond internal project capabilities.

## Internal vs External Boundaries
The project should respect that:

- internal monitoring and explanation are valuable;
- formal legal decisions and actions may require external professional counsel;
- the Advisor helps with awareness and understanding, not with full legal representation.

## Future Expansion
This file can later be expanded with:

- specific source lists;
- country- or region-specific official portals;
- curated professional resources;
- internal note structures for how to interpret certain types of changes.

## Final Principle
Legal and regulatory monitoring inside the Formula Finance project should be:

- structured;
- source-aware;
- cautious;
- focused on practical relevance;
- respectful of the limits between internal advisory support and formal legal practice.
