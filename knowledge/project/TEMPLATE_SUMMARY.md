# TEMPLATE SUMMARY

## Purpose
This file defines the standard structure for a summary inside the Formula Finance project.

Its purpose is to help turn longer materials, conversations, documents, or findings into clear short-form outputs that are easy to use.

## Main Principle
A summary should reduce complexity without losing the important meaning.

A good summary should answer:
- what this is about;
- what matters most;
- what the main findings are;
- what to do next, if action is needed.

## Standard Summary Structure

### 1. Title
A short, clear title describing what is being summarized.

Examples:
- client call summary;
- proposal summary;
- KPI review summary;
- legal update summary;
- reporting review summary.

### 2. Context
A brief note about the source or situation.

Examples:
- summary of a client conversation;
- summary of a report;
- summary of a legal update;
- summary of an internal discussion.

### 3. Main Points
List the most important points in a structured way.

This section should:
- focus on the core meaning;
- avoid unnecessary detail;
- be easy to scan quickly.

### 4. Key Findings or Conclusions
State the most important findings that should not be missed.

Examples:
- the client’s real pain point became clearer;
- proposal scope needs adjustment;
- KPI logic is still incomplete;
- a legal change may affect a reporting process.

### 5. Risks or Open Questions
List unresolved questions, uncertainties, or possible risks.

This helps prevent false clarity.

### 6. Recommended Next Step
If relevant, define what should happen next.

Examples:
- prepare proposal;
- clarify scope;
- route to Legal & Regulatory Advisor;
- update CRM record;
- request missing information.

## Quality Rules
A summary should be:
- short enough to read quickly;
- complete enough to be useful;
- structured;
- clear;
- non-chaotic.

It should avoid:
- raw transcript style;
- vague filler;
- repeating every detail;
- mixing fact and guess without distinction.

## Role Use
This template is useful for:
- Colleague;
- Coordinator;
- CRM Agent;
- Proposal Agent;
- Analyst;
- Legal & Regulatory Advisor;
- Metrics / KPI Agent.

## Final Principle
A summary should help people understand the essence of something quickly and act on it if needed.
