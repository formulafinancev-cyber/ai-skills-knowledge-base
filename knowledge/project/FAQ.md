# FAQ

## Purpose
This file collects frequently asked questions about Formula Finance and the way it works.

Its purpose is to help agents answer common questions consistently and clearly.

---

## 1. What is Formula Finance?

Formula Finance is a structured financial and analytical support initiative for businesses.

It focuses on:
- financial management visibility;
- management reporting;
- KPI and metrics logic;
- dashboards and analytical views;
- practical decision support.

It is designed to replace chaotic or fragmented information with clearer understanding and usable insights.

---

## 2. Is Formula Finance an accounting firm?

No.

Formula Finance is not a full accounting firm and does not aim to replace accounting providers.

It works on the intersection of:
- financial analysis;
- management reporting;
- KPI and analytics;
- operational visibility;
- decision support.

Accounting is about bookkeeping and compliance.  
Formula Finance is about understanding, structure, and visibility for decision makers.

---

## 3. Is Formula Finance a legal firm?

No.

Formula Finance is not a legal firm and does not provide full legal services.

However, there is a Legal & Regulatory Advisor role in the AI office that supports:
- regulatory monitoring;
- tax and legal update tracking;
- basic risk spotting;
- contract-oriented interpretation support.

This role acts as an advisory and monitoring layer, not as a replacement for formal legal counsel.

---

## 4. What kinds of problems does Formula Finance help solve?

Typical problem types include:
- weak financial visibility;
- inconsistent reporting;
- unclear KPI logic;
- difficulty interpreting numbers;
- chaotic spreadsheets;
- lack of structured management reporting;
- poor connection between data and decisions.

Formula Finance helps bring structure and visibility into these areas.

---

## 5. What does Formula Finance actually do in practice?

In practice, Formula Finance may:
- design management reporting structures;
- help define and organize KPIs;
- support dashboard and report logic;
- help interpret financial and operational results;
- structure planning and simple forecasting views;
- support management with clearer financial understanding.

---

## 6. Is this only about dashboards?

No.

Dashboards are only one part of the picture.

The core value is:
- structured thinking about finance and operations;
- making reporting and KPIs meaningful;
- improving management clarity.

Dashboards are a tool, not the full service.

---

## 7. How is pricing decided?

Pricing is not random.

It is based on:
- scope of work;
- complexity of the business case;
- reporting and KPI complexity;
- implementation effort;
- level of ongoing support.

For more detail, see the `PRICING.md` file.

---

## 8. Does Formula Finance replace existing staff?

No.

Formula Finance is not meant to replace:
- accounting staff;
- finance staff;
- management roles.

It is meant to support them by:
- improving reporting logic;
- clarifying KPIs;
- structuring analytical views;
- helping interpret results.

---

## 9. Is the system “only AI”?

No.

The AI office is an important part of the project architecture, but the underlying idea is broader:

- structured financial thinking;
- clear reporting systems;
- useful analytics;
- better decision-support processes.

AI is used as an instrument to support this, not as a marketing label.

---

## 10. Who is Formula Finance for?

Formula Finance is for businesses that:
- want better financial visibility;
- feel that their reporting is chaotic or weak;
- need better management control;
- want structured KPIs and dashboards;
- value practical, structured analytical help.

---

## 11. What is the main benefit for a business owner?

The main benefit is clearer understanding.

A business owner receives:
- structured reporting;
- structured KPIs;
- better visibility into what is happening;
- support in interpreting financial and operational results;
- a more confident basis for decisions.

---

## 12. How does the AI office fit into this?

The AI office is the internal operating system for Formula Finance.

It:
- helps structure work across roles;
- supports internal workflows;
- helps generate documents, proposals, and analytical outputs;
- keeps knowledge and context organized.

It is the internal operational layer behind the client-facing work.
