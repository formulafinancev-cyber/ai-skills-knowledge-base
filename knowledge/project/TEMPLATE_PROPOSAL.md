# TEMPLATE PROPOSAL

## Purpose
This file defines the standard structure for a proposal inside the Formula Finance project.

Its purpose is to make proposals:
- clear;
- structured;
- consistent;
- easier to assemble from reusable blocks.

## Main Principle
A proposal should help the client understand:
- their situation;
- the problem;
- the proposed support;
- the expected value;
- the approximate commercial logic;
- what happens next.

It should be serious, calm, and practical.

## Standard Proposal Structure

### 1. Title and Basic Info
- Proposal title  
- Client name  
- Date  

Optional short tagline that reflects the core focus (e.g., management reporting and visibility support).

---

### 2. Client Situation (Client Situation Block)
A concise description of the client’s current situation, based on the brief and conversations.

Focus on:
- how the business operates in general terms;
- current reporting and visibility state;
- context that matters for financial and analytical support.

This should show understanding, not guesswork.

---

### 3. Problem Definition (Problem Definition Block)
A clear description of the key problems the proposal addresses.

Examples:
- weak management reporting;
- unclear financial visibility;
- fragmented KPIs;
- difficulty turning data into decisions;
- lack of structured dashboards.

The language should be specific and practical.

---

### 4. Impact of the Current Situation (Impact Block)
Explain why the problem matters.

Examples:
- management decisions are based on incomplete information;
- leadership has low confidence in numbers;
- difficulties in seeing performance clearly;
- inability to quickly detect trends or risks.

This section connects problems to consequences.

---

### 5. Proposed Support Scope (Support Scope Block)
Describe what Formula Finance proposes to do.

Examples:
- design management reporting structure;
- define KPI logic;
- support dashboard and analytical view design;
- support planning and plan-vs-fact visibility;
- help interpret results and build decision-support habits.

This section should clearly connect to the earlier problem description.

---

### 6. Work Format (Work Format Block)
Describe how the work can be organized.

Possible elements:
- diagnostic phase;
- design / setup phase;
- initial reporting and KPI structure implementation;
- optional ongoing support phase.

The goal is to make the process feel concrete.

---

### 7. Deliverables (Deliverables Block)
List what the client will practically receive.

Examples:
- reporting structure description;
- KPI set and dictionary entries;
- initial dashboard or view logic;
- interpretation and usage guidelines;
- periodic summary format templates.

Deliverables should be realistic and meaningful.

---

### 8. Value for the Client (Value Block)
Explain the practical value.

Examples:
- clearer financial and operational visibility;
- more structured management reporting;
- better understanding of business performance;
- more confident decisions;
- less confusion around numbers.

Avoid exaggerated promises.

---

### 9. Case Relevance (Case Relevance Block)
Optionally include brief reference to relevant cases or patterns.

Examples:
- anonymized similar situation and outcome;
- pattern-based example showing how structured reporting changed visibility.

This is to increase credibility, not to oversell.

---

### 10. Pricing Logic (Pricing Logic Block)
Explain the pricing logic in simple terms.

If exact number is known:
- state it clearly;
- indicate what is included.

If exact number is not yet defined:
- explain pricing category (e.g., diagnostic, structured setup, ongoing support);
- explain factors that influence the final cost;
- state that a tailored figure can be provided after a more precise scope is confirmed.

This block should remain calm and transparent.

---

### 11. Next Steps (Next Step Block)
Explain what should happen if the client is interested.

Examples:
- “clarify scope in a short follow-up call”;
- “confirm the phase to start with (diagnostic / setup)”;
- “align on timeline and access to necessary data”.

The goal is to reduce friction and make the next move clear.

---

## Tone and Style
The proposal should:
- follow the brand voice (clear, structured, calm, professional, practical);
- avoid hype;
- stay focused on usefulness;
- be respectful and serious.

## Block Reuse
This template aligns directly with the blocks in `PROPOSAL_BLOCKS.md`:

- Client Situation Block;
- Problem Definition Block;
- Impact Block;
- Support Scope Block;
- Work Format Block;
- Deliverables Block;
- Value Block;
- Case Relevance Block;
- Pricing Logic Block;
- Next Step Block.

Proposal Agent should treat this as the default skeleton and adapt as needed for each client.

## Final Principle
A Formula Finance proposal should feel like:
- a thoughtful, structured response to a real business situation,
- not like a generic sales brochure.

It should help the client see where they are, what is being offered, and how it may help — in calm, clear language.
