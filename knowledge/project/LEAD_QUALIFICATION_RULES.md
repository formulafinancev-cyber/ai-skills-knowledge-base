# LEAD QUALIFICATION RULES

## Purpose
This file defines how lead qualification should be understood inside the Formula Finance project.

Its purpose is to help the system distinguish between weak leads, promising leads, and genuinely relevant opportunities.

## Main Principle
Not every lead is a real opportunity.

Lead qualification should help answer:
- is this business relevant;
- is there a visible problem that Formula Finance can help solve;
- is there enough commercial potential to continue;
- does this lead justify follow-up effort.

## Qualification Philosophy
Lead qualification should be practical, not abstract.

The system should look for signs that suggest:
- a real need;
- a relevant business context;
- a likely pain point;
- an actual fit with Formula Finance services.

Qualification should reduce noise and focus attention.

## Main Qualification Dimensions

### 1. Business Relevance
The lead should belong to a business context that could realistically benefit from:
- financial visibility support;
- management reporting;
- KPI logic;
- dashboarding;
- structured analytics;
- operational decision support.

If the business has no realistic fit for the service, qualification should remain low.

### 2. Problem Signals
A stronger lead usually shows signs such as:
- weak reporting visibility;
- poor KPI clarity;
- operational chaos;
- rapid growth with low structure;
- management complexity;
- unclear financial control;
- need for structured reporting or analytics.

The clearer the pain, the stronger the qualification.

### 3. Service Fit
The lead should have a visible fit with one or more Formula Finance service directions.

Examples:
- reporting structure fit;
- KPI fit;
- planning support fit;
- dashboard fit;
- management visibility fit;
- analytical support fit.

A lead may be interesting, but still not fit the service model well.

### 4. Commercial Potential
Qualification should consider whether the lead looks commercially realistic.

This may include:
- seriousness of the business;
- probable ability to pay;
- visible business activity;
- signs of management maturity;
- likelihood that structured support would be valued.

### 5. Actionability
A lead should be easier to qualify further if there is enough information to support action.

Examples:
- identifiable company;
- identifiable contact path;
- enough public context;
- clear next research step;
- possible outreach route.

A lead that cannot realistically move forward may remain weak even if the business seems interesting.

## Qualification Levels
Leads can be thought of through broad levels:

### Weak Lead
A weak lead has:
- limited visible fit;
- unclear need;
- low actionability;
- weak commercial signs;
- poor signal quality.

### Medium Lead
A medium lead has:
- some visible fit;
- some relevant signals;
- partial actionability;
- possible need;
- incomplete clarity.

### Strong Lead
A strong lead has:
- visible service fit;
- meaningful problem signals;
- practical actionability;
- clear business relevance;
- good potential for follow-up.

## Qualification Rule
A lead should not be treated as strong only because the company is large or well-known.

Qualification should be based on fit and signals, not image alone.

## Scout Rule
Scout should use these rules to prioritize discovery and first-pass filtering.

The goal of Scout is not to collect the largest number of names.  
The goal is to identify relevant leads with useful signs of fit.

## CRM Rule
CRM Agent should use qualification logic to help:
- prioritize pipeline attention;
- separate low-value from high-value leads;
- support cleaner follow-up decisions;
- identify which leads deserve proposal movement.

## Coordinator Rule
Coordinator should use qualification level to help decide:
- what to do next;
- whether follow-up is justified;
- whether the lead should move toward proposal logic;
- whether more research is needed first.

## Future Expansion
This file can later be expanded with:
- explicit scoring dimensions;
- weighted qualification criteria;
- red flags;
- disqualification rules;
- by-industry qualification patterns;
- source-specific qualification logic.

## Final Principle
Lead qualification should help Formula Finance focus commercial effort where:
- service fit is real;
- pain is visible;
- follow-up is justified;
- the opportunity is worth pursuing.
