# CASE LIBRARY

## Purpose
This file defines how case materials should be understood and used inside the Formula Finance project.

Its purpose is to create a structured base for future case examples, proof points, proposal support, sales communication, and content references.

## Main Principle
A case is not just a story.

A case is a structured example of a business problem, the work performed, and the value created.

Cases help agents explain:
- what Formula Finance does;
- what kinds of problems it can solve;
- what kind of outcomes are realistic;
- why the service is valuable.

## Why Cases Matter
Cases are important because they help:
- make the service concrete;
- support proposals;
- support sales communication;
- support trust-building;
- support content creation;
- support internal positioning.

Without cases, communication becomes too abstract.

## What a Good Case Should Show
A useful case should show:
- the business context;
- the original problem;
- the analytical or reporting issue;
- the approach taken;
- the type of structure introduced;
- the resulting business clarity or benefit.

The goal is not only to describe activity.  
The goal is to show practical value.

## Typical Elements of a Case
A future case entry may include:
- client type or business type;
- initial problem;
- source of confusion or weak visibility;
- what Formula Finance helped structure;
- what changed after the work;
- what management gained in understanding or control.

## Acceptable Case Types
Case materials may include:
- formal client cases;
- anonymized cases;
- composite examples based on recurring patterns;
- internal proof-of-concept examples;
- structured “before / after” reporting situations.

Not every case must be public in the same form, but all cases should remain useful for internal work.

## Confidentiality Rule
Cases must respect confidentiality.

If a real client example is sensitive, it should be:
- anonymized;
- generalized;
- stripped of sensitive identifying details;
- rewritten in a safe business form.

Agents should never expose confidential business details casually.

## Proposal Use
Proposal Agent should use case logic to:
- make offers more credible;
- show relevant examples;
- match the right type of case to the right client situation;
- reduce abstract selling language.

Cases in proposals should help the client see practical relevance.

## Content Use
Content Strategist and Publisher may use case logic to:
- generate educational content ideas;
- explain common business problems;
- show how structured reporting improves decision quality;
- create trust-building materials.

Cases used in content should remain clear, practical, and non-hyped.

## CRM and Scout Use
CRM Agent and Scout may use case logic to:
- understand what kinds of leads are a good fit;
- recognize recurring pain patterns;
- support better qualification;
- connect prospect needs with relevant proof logic.

## Case Quality Rule
A useful case should avoid:
- vague success language;
- empty marketing phrases;
- exaggerated promises;
- artificial certainty.

It should instead emphasize:
- context;
- structure;
- business usefulness;
- practical clarity.

## Current State Rule
This file may initially serve as a structure guide even if the full set of cases is not yet filled in.

That is acceptable.

It is better to define the case logic early than to let future case material become chaotic.

## Future Expansion
This file can later be expanded with:
- individual case entries;
- anonymized client examples;
- by-industry cases;
- by-problem-type cases;
- proposal-ready mini cases;
- content-ready case fragments.

## Final Principle
The case library should help Formula Finance communicate real value through structured examples.

Cases should make the company more concrete, more credible, and more useful in communication.
