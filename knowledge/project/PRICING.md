# PRICING

## Purpose
This file defines the pricing logic of Formula Finance at a high level.

Its purpose is not necessarily to store final price numbers only.  
Its purpose is to define how pricing should be understood and discussed inside the project.

## Main Principle
Pricing should be structured, explainable, and business-appropriate.

Formula Finance should avoid random or improvised pricing logic.

Pricing communication should support:
- clarity;
- trust;
- seriousness;
- practical fit to client needs.

## Pricing Philosophy
Formula Finance pricing should reflect:
- scope of work;
- complexity of the business case;
- level of analytical depth;
- amount of reporting structure required;
- degree of customization;
- expected implementation effort;
- ongoing support needs.

Pricing should not be treated as a one-size-fits-all flat answer unless the service really is standardized.

## General Pricing Logic
Pricing may depend on factors such as:
- one-time versus recurring work;
- diagnostic versus implementation-oriented work;
- simple versus complex reporting structure;
- single-business versus multi-unit structure;
- level of management reporting depth;
- dashboard and KPI complexity;
- amount of adaptation required.

## Pricing Categories
Formula Finance pricing can be understood through broad categories.

### 1. Entry / Diagnostic Work
Used for:
- first review;
- initial assessment;
- light analytical diagnosis;
- identifying key problems and next steps.

This category is useful when the client is not yet ready for a full implementation scope.

### 2. Structured Analytical Setup
Used for:
- establishing reporting logic;
- designing financial views;
- setting KPI logic;
- creating structured management analytics.

This category is more serious than an initial diagnostic and usually involves actual system thinking and business structuring.

### 3. Customized Ongoing Support
Used for:
- recurring analytical support;
- recurring reporting interpretation;
- ongoing dashboard or KPI support;
- periodic management visibility support.

This category applies when Formula Finance becomes part of the client’s regular decision-support environment.

### 4. Custom / Special Scope Work
Used for:
- unusual business cases;
- large analytical complexity;
- non-standard structure;
- expanded management logic;
- highly tailored support needs.

This category should usually be discussed individually.

## Communication Rules
Pricing should be communicated in a way that is:
- calm;
- professional;
- clear;
- non-defensive;
- structured.

The goal is not only to state a price.  
The goal is to explain what level of value, structure, and work the client is receiving.

## Proposal Rule
Proposal Agent should use this pricing file as a logic reference.

If exact pricing numbers are not yet fixed, Proposal Agent should not invent hard numbers casually.

Instead, it should:
- refer to pricing logic;
- define likely pricing category;
- explain scope-related pricing reasoning;
- identify where a tailored estimate is needed.

## CRM Rule
CRM Agent should treat pricing as sensitive structured information.

It should help track:
- pricing stage;
- discussed level of scope;
- whether a custom quote is needed;
- whether the lead is still at diagnostic level or already at implementation level.

## Colleague Rule
Colleague may explain pricing philosophy in broad terms, but should not invent precise commercial terms when they are not defined.

## Future Expansion
This file can later be expanded with:
- concrete pricing packages;
- package boundaries;
- discount logic;
- recurring support tiers;
- proposal-ready commercial ranges;
- negotiation notes.

## Final Principle
Pricing inside Formula Finance should be treated as structured business logic, not improvisation.

All agents should preserve seriousness, clarity, and consistency when working with pricing-related topics.
